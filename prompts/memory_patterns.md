# Memory Storage Patterns

When debriefing a meeting, store each piece of information as a separate memory.
Phrase memories for optimal future semantic recall — include names, companies, dates, and topics.

## Categories and Examples

### decision
Store agreements, approvals, and strategic choices.
- "Meeting with Alex Chen (Acme Corp) on 2026-03-25: Agreed to extend partnership for 12 months at $200K annually."
- "Decision from 2026-03-25 call with Sarah Lee (TechCo): Will use their API v3 instead of v2 for the integration."

### fact
Store action items, deadlines, and objective information.
- "Action item from 2026-03-25 meeting with Alex Chen: Marvin needs to send revised contract by 2026-03-28."
- "Acme Corp's fiscal year ends in June. Budget decisions happen in April."
- "Alex Chen mentioned their team is growing from 5 to 12 people in Q2 2026."

### preference
Store communication preferences, working styles, and personal notes.
- "Alex Chen prefers async communication over meetings. Learned 2026-03-25."
- "Sarah Lee likes to start meetings with a personal check-in before business. Noted 2026-03-25."
- "Acme Corp team prefers quarterly reviews instead of monthly. Agreed 2026-03-25."

### entity
Store role information, relationships, and organizational context.
- "Alex Chen is VP of Partnerships at Acme Corp. Reports to CEO Jane Doe. Has budget authority up to $500K."
- "TechCo's main competitor is BuildCo. They're sensitive about feature comparisons."
- "Acme Corp office is in Austin, TX. Alex works remotely from NYC."

## Phrasing Guidelines

1. Always include the person's full name and company
2. Always include dates (use ISO format: 2026-03-25)
3. Be specific — "agreed to $200K" not "agreed to a deal"
4. One concept per memory — don't combine decisions with preferences
5. Write in third person for entity/preference, first person for action items
6. Include enough context that the memory makes sense 6 months from now
